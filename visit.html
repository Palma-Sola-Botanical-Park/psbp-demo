What to see

Rare Palm Collection

The park's roots as a county palm nursery are visible everywhere. Rare specimens flourish in our protected frost-free microclimate — varieties that simply can't survive elsewhere in Manatee County.

Exotic Fruit Trail

Planted by the Manatee Rare Fruit Council in 2008 and maintained ever since. Star fruit, dragon fruit, fig, guava, jackfruit, lychee, persimmon, pomelo, sapodilla, tamarind, and more than a dozen others. Take the slow walk. Read the signs. Half the people who visit never find it.

Butterfly Garden

Native Florida host plants for native Florida butterflies. Come in the morning. Bring kids. Bring a camera. Give it ten quiet minutes and you'll see something.

Three Lakes

Wood storks, great blue herons, anhingas, turtles. The lakes are the quiet heart of the park. Log what you see on iNaturalist.

Hidden Garden Nursery

Tucked behind the butterfly patch near the west entrance. Volunteer-grown plants propagated from our own grounds — reasonably priced, every dollar stays in the park.

Open Tuesdays and Saturdays, 9am–Noon · October through May

Art in the Park

The Galleria building hosts a rotating exhibit of local amateur and professional artists — a different artist or guild every month. Watercolor, oil, pastel, photography, sculpture. All work is for sale, and a generous portion of proceeds supports the park. New artist receptions are typically held the last Sunday of the month.

If you're an artist interested in exhibiting, contact us.

Group Tours

For groups of 10 or more, please call our office at (941) 761-2866 to schedule. Suggested donation is $10 per person to support the park.

Photography

Recreational photographers are welcome — share your photos and tag us! For professional or commercial photography, please contact the office first to discuss our policy.
