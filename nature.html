Plants & Nature

We don't know everything that lives here. That's not a failing — it's the whole point. Every visit is a chance to document something, add to the record, and contribute to real science. iNaturalist makes that possible for anyone with a phone.

How to contribute

  1. Download the iNaturalist app (free, iOS or Android)
  2. Walk through the park and photograph anything interesting — plants, insects, birds, fungi, anything
  3. Submit your observation. The community and AI tools will help identify it
  4. Once two experts agree, it becomes a verified "Research Grade" observation
  5. Your data is permanently part of the park's scientific record

What you'll find here

Rare Palm Collection

The park began as a county palm nursery in 1990, and the collection reflects that history. Many specimens are rare outside of dedicated collections. This protected microclimate — notably frost-free — allows species to thrive here that couldn't survive anywhere else in the county.

Exotic Fruit Trail

Planted and maintained by the Manatee Rare Fruit Council since 2008. Star fruit, dragon fruit, jackfruit, lychee, sapodilla, tamarind, persimmon, guava, loquat, Surinam cherry, white sapote, ylang ylang — over two dozen species on a single walk. Most visitors walk right past it without knowing.

Butterfly Garden

Native host plants specifically chosen for Florida's butterfly species. Come in the morning. Bring a camera. Give the kids time to look carefully.

Three Lakes

The park's lakes support wading birds, turtles, and aquatic plants. Wood storks, great blue herons, and anhingas are regular visitors. Log what you see on iNaturalist.

Florida Native Plants

The park actively expands its native Florida plant collection. Volunteers propagate cuttings in the Hidden Garden Nursery and transplant them throughout the grounds. Ongoing iNaturalist observation helps us track what's thriving and what's finding its way in on its own.
