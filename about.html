About Us

The story of how a surplus county nursery became Manatee County's only botanical park.

Our History

Palma Sola Botanical Park sits in the "Old Palma Sola" section of northwest Bradenton — an area with a rich history stretching from the Timucuan Indians through the Spanish Conquistadors to the present.

In 1990, local citizens and community groups looked at a 10-acre parcel of surplus county land — the former Manatee County Nursery — and saw something worth saving. The Bradenton Herald ran editorials. Neighbors organized. A movement formed around a simple idea: this land should be a botanical park, open to everyone, free forever.

In 1993, the Palma Sola Botanical Park Foundation, Inc. was incorporated as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. In 1996, a formal agreement with Manatee County Government established the park as a joint venture between the county parks department and the nonprofit foundation.

Since then, the park has grown steadily — adding the Galleria building in 2000 for weddings, events, and art exhibitions, the Manatee Rare Fruit Council's exotic fruit collection in 2008, and most recently a new composite deck and ramp funded by community donations.

Today the park holds collections of rare palms, subtropical plants, and exotic fruit trees that thrive in its uniquely frost-free microclimate. It is open seven days a week, free of charge, supported entirely by donations, memberships, and venue rentals. No government subsidy. No entrance fee. Never has been, never will be.

Our Mission

To serve as a unique community resource dedicated to providing an interactive learning environment that fosters an appreciation of nature and landscaping, preserves our natural resources, and promotes environmental education.
