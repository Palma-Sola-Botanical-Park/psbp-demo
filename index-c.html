About this place

Ten frost-free acres, fighting for themselves since 1990.

In 1990, neighbors looked at a surplus county nursery and said: no. Not a parking lot. Not a development. A park. They had no money and no blueprint, only the conviction that Manatee County needed this green space.

We don't charge admission. We receive no government funding. What you see here exists because people keep showing up.

Today the park holds collections of rare palms, an exotic fruit trail with jackfruit and lychee and dragon fruit, a butterfly garden, three lakes, and a volunteer nursery. iNaturalist observations from the park document over 460 species — and we're still learning what lives here.
